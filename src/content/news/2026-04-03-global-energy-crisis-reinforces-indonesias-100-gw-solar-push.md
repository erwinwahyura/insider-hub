## Summary
Prabowo accelerates Indonesia's 100 GW solar target within 3 years due to Middle East geopolitical tensions threatening energy security, though regulatory uncertainty and PLN procurement delays continue to stall implementation.

## Key Points
• **100 GW solar target**: Announced for next 3 years (currently only 1.7% of energy mix vs coal's 36%)
• **Implementation hurdles**: Regulatory uncertainty, financing gaps, land acquisition challenges, and PLN's slow procurement blocking power purchase agreements
• **Economic impact**: Could save US$2B in diesel imports + US$1B in subsidies, but tariff framework still under discussion

## Portfolio Impact
🔴 **ITMG/ADRO (Coal)**: **Negative** — Solar push threatens coal dominance (36% → lower). Regulatory hurdles may delay impact, but long-term trend unfavorable for coal plays.

🟢 **PGEO (Geothermal)**: **Positive** — Already part of renewable mix (~16% with hydro). Energy security focus benefits established renewable infrastructure players.

🟡 **Sector rotation watch**: If 100 GW target gains traction, coal stocks face structural headwinds. Monitor PLN procurement announcements for timing.
