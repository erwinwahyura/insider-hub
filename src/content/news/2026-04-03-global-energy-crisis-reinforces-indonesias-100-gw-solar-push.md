## Summary
The global energy crisis is strengthening Indonesia's commitment to its ambitious 100 GW solar power target, though implementation challenges and infrastructure hurdles remain significant obstacles.

## Key Points
• Indonesia's 100 GW solar target gaining momentum amid global energy crisis
• Implementation hurdles persist — grid infrastructure, financing, land acquisition
• Energy transition creating winners/losers: coal (ITMG, ADRO) vs renewables (PGEO)
• Policy execution risk high; targets may slip without structural reforms

## Portfolio Impact
🔴 **ITMG / ADRO**: Solar push at scale threatens long-term coal demand narrative. Domestic coal usage may face pressure as solar capacity expands.

🟢 **PGEO**: Geothermal well-positioned as baseload renewable complementary to intermittent solar. Pertamina's integrated energy play benefits from diversification narrative.

**Position Check**: PGEO (205 lots at 1,011) offers energy transition exposure without coal's structural decline risk. ITMG (28,100 avg) faces medium-term headwinds from renewable shift.
