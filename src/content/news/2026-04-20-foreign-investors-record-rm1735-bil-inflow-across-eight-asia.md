## Summary

Foreign investors pumped RM17.35 billion (approximately $3.7 billion USD) into eight Asian markets last week, signaling renewed confidence in the region's emerging markets despite global uncertainty.

## Key Points

• **Significant capital inflow**: RM17.35 billion ($3.7B USD) flowed into eight Asian markets — the largest weekly inflow in recent months
• **Regional breadth**: Inflows spread across multiple markets, indicating broad-based confidence rather than single-country speculation
• **Indonesia implication**: Strong regional flows typically benefit Indonesia (IDX) as foreign investors increase EM allocations
• **Context**: Inflows come amid Iran war concerns and US-China tensions, suggesting investors view Asian markets as relative safe havens

## Portfolio Impact

🟢 **Positive for IDX** — Large foreign inflows into Asian markets typically correlate with increased foreign buying on the Indonesia Stock Exchange (IDX). This could support:
- Your **PTPS** position (CPO/plantation sector benefits from foreign risk appetite)
- Your **ESSA** position (chemical/fertilizer sector often sees foreign interest)
- Overall IHSG index stability during volatile global conditions
