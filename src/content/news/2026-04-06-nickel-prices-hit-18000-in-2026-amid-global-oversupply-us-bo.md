## Summary
Nickel prices hover around $18,000/tonne in 2026 as global structural oversupply of 200-250,000 tonnes caps sustained upside, though Indonesia's December 2025 production controls have stabilized prices from lower levels.

## Key Points
• **Price stabilization**: Indonesia's production cuts (announced Dec 23, 2025) lifted nickel from lows to ~$17,000-18,000/tonne range
• **Oversupply persists**: Global market remains oversupplied by 200-250,000 tonnes, limiting price recovery potential
• **US supply chain**: Domestic US critical mineral projects gaining attention as alternative to Indonesian supply dominance

## Portfolio Impact
🔴 **ANTM/INCO (Nickel miners)**: **Negative** — Oversupply caps nickel price upside despite production cuts. Structural headwinds continue for Indonesian nickel plays.

🟡 **ESSA (Ammonia/Fertilizer)**: **Neutral** — Not directly nickel-exposed, but industrial metal weakness signals broader commodity caution.

🟢 **Entry watch**: If nickel breaks below $16,000, may present accumulation opportunity in quality miners before EV demand recovery.
