## Summary
Turkey's sovereign risk perception improved markedly as 5-year CDS premiums dropped 17 basis points to 268 bps following the US-Iran temporary ceasefire announcement, while Turkish stocks gained nearly 5%.

## Key Points
• **CDS relief**: Turkey's 5-year credit default swap premium fell 17 bps to 268 bps after US-Iran truce
• **Stock rally**: BIST-100 gained ~5% midday on reduced geopolitical risk
• **Hormuz status**: Strait remains closed but truce signals potential reopening; Iran demands transit revenues cover war damages

## Portfolio Impact
🟢 **PGEO (Geothermal/Energy)**: **Positive** — Reduced Mideast risk premium lowers energy security concerns. Iran truce + potential Hormuz reopening reduces oil price volatility, stabilizing Indonesia's energy import costs and supporting Pertamina plays.

🟡 **Regional macro**: **Cautiously positive** — Truce is temporary (2 weeks) but opens negotiation window. If sustained, reduces energy crisis premium across ASEAN markets.

🟢 **Risk-on signal**: Turkey CDS drop + equity rally suggests global risk appetite improving — bullish for emerging market energy infrastructure plays.
