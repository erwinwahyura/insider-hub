## Summary
The Iran conflict highlights Indonesia's vulnerability to global energy supply shocks, reinforcing the urgency of accelerating the country's energy transition away from fossil fuel dependence.

## Key Points
• Iran war creating global energy supply disruptions and price volatility
• Indonesia's fossil fuel dependency exposes economy to external shocks
• Energy transition acceleration seen as national security imperative
• Coal and oil price volatility impacting Indonesian energy sector

## Portfolio Impact
🟡 **NEUTRAL** — Mixed implications for portfolio positions:
- **ITMG/ADRO (Coal)**: Short-term price volatility from supply disruptions, but long-term transition pressure remains
- **PGEO (Geothermal)**: Positive — energy transition narrative supports geothermal as clean baseload alternative
- **ESSA/PTPS**: Minimal direct impact, but higher energy costs could affect industrial margins

The Iran conflict reinforces the dual nature of Indonesia's energy dilemma: near-term reliance on coal/oil for energy security vs. long-term transition imperative. PGEO's geothermal positioning benefits from transition acceleration narrative.
