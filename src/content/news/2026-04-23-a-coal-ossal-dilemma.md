## Summary
Indonesia faces a strategic dilemma balancing its coal-dependent energy infrastructure with global pressure to accelerate the energy transition, creating uncertainty for coal producers like ITMG and ADRO while presenting opportunities for geothermal players like PGEO.

## Key Points
• Indonesia remains heavily dependent on coal for electricity generation (~60% of energy mix)
• Global climate commitments and carbon border adjustments pressure coal phase-down
• Government targets 34% renewable energy by 2030, but coal expansion continues
• Geothermal (PGEO) positioned as key transition fuel with 24GW potential
• Coal producers face long-term demand uncertainty despite short-term price strength
