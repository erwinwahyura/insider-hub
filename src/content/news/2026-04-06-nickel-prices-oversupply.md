## Summary
Nickel prices have declined to $18,000/tonne in 2026 as global oversupply continues to pressure markets, while the US accelerates domestic supply chain development to reduce import dependence.

## Key Points
• **Price Level:** Nickel at $18,000/tonne — down from 2024 peaks above $30,000
• **Oversupply:** Global production exceeding demand, particularly from Indonesian and Philippine mines
• **US Response:** Domestic supply chain investments to secure critical mineral access
• **Market Impact:** Lower prices squeeze margins for nickel producers and stainless steel makers

## Portfolio Impact
🔴 **Negative for ANTM, INCO, ESSA**

Your nickel/chemical exposure via ESSA (Ammonia/Chemical) and indirect nickel holdings may face continued margin pressure. ANTM and INCO are direct nickel plays facing headwinds from oversupply pricing.

**Action:** Monitor Q1 2026 production reports for cost guidance adjustments. If nickel stays sub-$20K, expect downward earnings revisions.
