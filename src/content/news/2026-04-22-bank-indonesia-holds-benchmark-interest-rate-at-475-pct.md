## Summary
Bank Indonesia (BI) maintained its benchmark interest rate (BI Rate) at 4.75% during the April 2026 meeting, keeping rates steady for the eighth consecutive month as inflation remains within target and the rupiah stabilizes.

## Key Points
• **Rate Hold**: BI Rate unchanged at 4.75%, Deposit Facility at 4.00%, Lending Facility at 5.50%
• **Rationale**: Inflation within 2.5% ± 1% target range; rupiah exchange rate relatively stable; global uncertainty remains elevated
• **Forward Guidance**: BI signals "cautious" stance — prioritizing stability over growth acceleration
• **Macro Context**: Decision aligns with expectations; no pressure to cut despite regional peers easing

## Portfolio Impact
🟡 **Neutral** — Rate hold was fully expected by markets. No surprise = no immediate price impact.

**Banking (BBRI, BBCA)**: Stable rates support net interest margins (NIM). No pressure on bank profitability from rate cuts. Deposit costs remain manageable.

**Commodity Plays (ITMG, ADRO, ESSA, PGEO)**: Rupiah stability (IDR ~16,200-16,400/USD) is supportive. No urgent need for BI to defend currency = no aggressive tightening. Neutral for commodity exporters.

**Key Watch**: BI Governor Perry Warjiyo signaled BI is "ready to adjust" if inflation or rupiah pressures emerge. Watch for:
- May inflation print (due early May)
- Fed policy trajectory (if Fed cuts, BI may follow with lag)
- Rupiah movement vs USD
